## [4] hash2 (total votes: 17)  
  
## Conclusion Message:  
[hash2] Review Report — Matt Borland (Review Manager), Dec 2024. The review of Hash2 by Peter Dimov and Christian Mazakas ran from December 7 through December 15, 2024. Bottom line: **The library is ACCEPTED.** Discussion was largely positive; reviewers appreciated that it implements the "Types don't know #" hashing proposal. The report summarized discussion points (e.g. std::span support, C++11 vs newer interfaces) and recommendations. Multiple reviewers submitted ACCEPT (e.g. Vinnie Falco, Christopher Kormanyos, Richard Hodges); no vote tally was given in the report.  
https://lists.boost.org/archives/list/boost@lists.boost.org/message/QBPJPEEIEYY2BO7C6IJ6ZXV2LGSZG62S/

## [5] proto (total votes: 16)  
  
## Conclusion Message:  
Hartmut Kaiser (review manager) announced: "Proto got 16 reviews (That's well above average! Thanks to all for participating), out of which 15 have been positive. The one 'No' essentially stated that the library, even if doubtless very useful, shouldn't be accepted yet, mainly because there has to be done more work in the area of formalizing the problem domain. In my opinion this is an overwhelming result and I'm whole heartily agreeing with the majority of the voters. Proto is accepted as a Boost library." Author: Eric Niebler. Expression-template DSL library; used by Xpressive, Spirit-2, and planned Phoenix/Lambda unification. April 2008.  
https://lists.boost.org/archives/list/boost@lists.boost.org/message/FQVMZN4QI5OB2U5KHB2LN7GJGUHBAWAP/
